## EU MDR Documentation Requirements

### Technical Documentation (Annex II and III)

**Annex II - Technical Documentation (Class I, IIa, IIb, III):**

**Section 1: Device Description and Specification**
- General description of device
- Intended purpose and clinical benefits
- Risk class and justification
- Novel features (if applicable)
- Device variants and accessories
- Labeling, IFU, promotional materials
- Previous and similar generations

**Section 2: Information Supplied by Manufacturer**
- Labels and instructions for use
- Patient implant card (if applicable)
- Summary of safety and clinical performance (SSCP) for class III and implantables

**Section 3: Design and Manufacturing**
- Complete design specifications
- Manufacturing sites and processes
- Sterilization and microbiology
- Raw material specifications
- Subcontractors and suppliers

**Section 4: General Safety and Performance Requirements**
- Checklist of applicable GSPR (Annex I)
- Solutions adopted for each requirement
- Standards applied (harmonized or other)
- Justification for non-application of requirements

**Section 5: Benefit-Risk Analysis and Risk Management**
- Complete risk management file (ISO 14971)
- Residual risks and risk-benefit analysis
- Risk management report

**Section 6: Product Verification and Validation**
- Pre-clinical evaluation (bench testing)
- Animal studies (if conducted)
- Clinical evaluation (Annex XIV)
- Post-market clinical follow-up (PMCF) plan

**Annex III - Technical Documentation on Post-Market Surveillance:**
- Post-market surveillance plan (PMS plan)
- Post-market surveillance report (PMSR) - annual for class IIa/IIb/III
- Periodic safety update report (PSUR) - for class IIa/IIb/III
- Post-market clinical follow-up (PMCF) evaluation report

**Documentation Maintenance:**
- Technical documentation updated throughout device lifecycle
- Changes documented and notified to Notified Body if applicable
- Documentation available for at least 10 years (15 for implantables) after last device placed on market

### Clinical Evaluation (Annex XIV)

**Clinical Evaluation Report (CER) Requirements:**
- Device description and specifications
- Clinical background (state of art)
- Literature search and review (systematic)
- Clinical investigation data
- Analysis of clinical data and benefit-risk determination
- Conclusions on compliance with GSPR

**Clinical Evidence Pathways:**
- Clinical investigation data (company-sponsored studies)
- Literature data (demonstration of equivalence to similar device)
- Combination of investigation and literature data

**PMCF Evaluation Report:**
- Continuation of clinical evaluation
- Analysis of post-market clinical data
- Confirmation of safety and performance
- Identification of previously unknown side effects
- Continuous benefit-risk assessment

### Quality Management System Documentation (Article 10)

**Required QMS Documentation per EU MDR:**
- Quality policy and objectives
- Organizational structure and responsibilities
- Risk management procedures
- Design and development procedures
- Production and process control procedures
- Corrective and preventive action procedures
- Post-market surveillance procedures
- Management review procedures

## FDA Documentation Requirements

### Device Master Record (DMR)

**21 CFR 820.181 - DMR Requirements:**

Complete compilation of records containing procedures and specifications for a finished device:

**Device Specifications:**
- Device drawings and schematics
- Composition, formulation, components
- Software specifications and architecture
- Manufacturing specifications
- Quality assurance specifications and procedures
- Packaging and labeling specifications
- Installation, maintenance, servicing procedures

**Manufacturing Process Specifications:**
- Process flow diagrams
- Process parameters and controls
- Equipment specifications and qualifications
- Process validation protocols and reports
- Environmental controls (cleanroom specifications)

**Quality Control Procedures:**
- In-process inspection and testing
- Final inspection and testing
- Acceptance and rejection criteria
- Measurement and test equipment specifications
- Sampling plans and procedures

**Component and Material Specifications:**
- Raw material specifications
- Component supplier information
- Material certificates of conformity
- Traceability requirements

**Labeling Specifications:**
- Label artwork and copy
- Labeling procedures
- Serialization and lot numbering
- Instructions for use (IFU)

### Device History Record (DHR)

**21 CFR 820.184 - DHR Requirements:**

Compilation of records containing production history of a finished device:

**DHR Must Include:**
- Date of manufacture
- Quantity manufactured
- Quantity released for distribution
- Acceptance records showing device meets specifications
- Primary identification label and labeling used
- UDI (Unique Device Identification)
- Any device identification(s) and control number(s)

**Production Records:**
- Manufacturing lot or batch numbers
- Component lot numbers and traceability
- Manufacturing steps performed
- In-process inspection results
- Final inspection and testing results
- Calibration records for measurement equipment used
- Signatures of operators who performed work

**Quality Control Records:**
- Test results and data
- Operator signatures and dates
- Acceptance or rejection decisions
- Disposition of nonconforming product

**Release Authorization:**
- Signature and date of individual(s) authorized to release device
- Release criteria verification

**Retention:** Retained for period of time equivalent to design and expected life of device, but not less than 2 years from date of release for distribution.

### Design History File (DHF)

**21 CFR 820.30(j) - Design Controls:**

Contains or references documentation necessary to demonstrate that design control requirements were met:

**DHF Contents:**
- Design and development plan
- Design input requirements
- Design output specifications
- Design review records
- Design verification protocols and reports
- Design validation protocols and reports
- Design transfer documentation
- Design change documentation

**Design Inputs:**
- Intended use and user requirements
- Regulatory requirements
- Performance requirements
- Safety requirements
- Interface requirements (hardware/software)
- Compatibility requirements
- Reliability requirements

**Design Outputs:**
- Device specifications
- Manufacturing specifications
- Software design specifications
- Test methods and acceptance criteria
- Labeling and IFU
- Installation and servicing procedures

**Design Review Records:**
- Review meeting minutes
- Attendees and roles
- Issues identified and resolutions
- Action items and follow-up
- Review conclusions and approval

**Verification and Validation:**
- Verification protocols and reports (design outputs meet inputs)
- Validation protocols and reports (device meets user needs)
- Test data and analysis
- Statistical analysis (if applicable)
- Clinical evaluation data

**Design Changes:**
- Change requests and justification
- Change review and approval
- Verification and validation of changes
- Implementation records

### FDA Submission Documentation

**510(k) Premarket Notification Contents:**
- Device description and specifications
- Indications for use statement
- Predicate device comparison (substantial equivalence)
- Performance testing (bench, animal, clinical)
- Biocompatibility assessment
- Software documentation (if applicable)
- Sterilization validation (if applicable)
- Electromagnetic compatibility/electrical safety
- Labeling (draft labels and IFU)
- Conclusion of substantial equivalence

**PMA (Premarket Approval) Contents:**
- Complete device description
- Indications for use
- Alternative practices and procedures
- Marketing history outside US
- Summary of studies
- Nonclinical laboratory studies
- Clinical investigations (IDE studies)
- Justification of single investigator (if applicable)
- Bibliography and references
- Sample of device
- Proposed labeling
- Environmental assessment (if significant impact)
- Manufacturing information
- Quality system information

**De Novo Classification Request:**
- Device description
- Proposed classification
- Indications for use
- Summary of reasons for De Novo
- Comparison to existing devices
- Description of risks and proposed mitigations
- Performance testing demonstrating safety and effectiveness
- Proposed special controls
- Proposed labeling

## 21 CFR Part 11 Electronic Records and Signatures

### Electronic Records Requirements (21 CFR Part 11 Subpart B)

**Validation (§11.10(a)):**
- Validate systems to ensure accuracy, reliability, consistent intended performance
- Ability to generate accurate and complete copies of records
- Retained in both human readable and electronic form

**Audit Trails (§11.10(e)):**
- Secure, computer-generated, time-stamped audit trails
- Independent of user who made the change
- Document and record all operator entries and actions
- Cannot be altered or deleted by operator

**System Security (§11.10):**
- Limit system access to authorized individuals
- Use of operational system checks
- Use of authority checks to ensure proper authorization
- Use of device (e.g., token) checks for authorized use
- Education and training on system security
- Written policies holding individuals accountable

**Data Integrity (§11.10(c)):**
- Use of secure, computer-generated, time-stamped audit trails
- Documentation of sequence of events
- Ability to generate accurate and complete copies
- Protection of records from loss, alteration, or damage

**Backup and Recovery (§11.10(b)):**
- Protection of records to enable accurate and ready retrieval
- Regular backup procedures
- Disaster recovery procedures
- Testing of backup and recovery procedures

**System Documentation (§11.10(k)):**
- Written policies and procedures for system operation
- Documentation of development and validation
- User manuals and training materials
- Standard operating procedures for system use
- Change control documentation

### Electronic Signatures Requirements (21 CFR Part 11 Subpart C)

**General Requirements (§11.50):**
- Signed electronic records shall contain information associated with signing including:
  - Printed name of signer
  - Date and time of signature
  - Meaning of signature (e.g., review, approval, authorship)

**Signature Components (§11.100):**
- Unique to one individual
- Not reused or reassigned
- Combination of:
  - Identification code (username)
  - Password
  - Biometric (optional)
  - Token or smartcard (optional)

**Password Controls (§11.300):**
- Unique to each individual
- Periodically checked, recalled, or revised
- Following loss management procedures
- Use of transaction safeguards to prevent unauthorized use
- Device checks to prevent unauthorized execution

**Signature Manifestation (§11.200):**
- Signed electronic record must clearly indicate:
  - Fact that signature has been applied
  - Printed name of signer
  - Date and time signature executed
  - Meaning of signature
- Signature information subject to same controls as record

**Signature/Record Linking (§11.70):**
- Electronic signatures and handwritten signatures executed to electronic records
- Shall be linked to respective records
- Cannot be excised, copied, or transferred by ordinary means
- Falsification of signature shall be equivalent to falsification of paper record

### Implementation Requirements

**System Validation:**
- Installation Qualification (IQ)
- Operational Qualification (OQ)
- Performance Qualification (PQ)
- Traceability matrix (requirements to tests)
- Validation report and approval

**Standard Operating Procedures:**
- System access and security
- Electronic signature procedures

- Audit trail review
- System administration
- Backup and recovery
- Change control
- Disaster recovery

**Training Requirements:**
- Initial system training before use
- Training on policies and procedures
- Training on security requirements
- Training records maintained
- Periodic refresher training

**Periodic Review:**
- Annual review of system controls
- Audit trail review
- User access review
- Password management review
- Deviation investigations
- CAPA for system issues

## International Market Documentation

### Health Canada Requirements

**Medical Device Regulations (SOR/98-282):**
- Medical Device License (MDL) application documentation
- Device classification and justification
- Quality Management System certification (ISO 13485)
- Risk management documentation
- Clinical evidence (if required)
- Labeling in English and French
- Establishment License for manufacturers/importers

**Quality Management System:**
- ISO 13485 certification by recognized Registrar
- Canadian-specific labeling requirements
- Medical Device Problem Reporting (incident reporting)
- Medical Device Recall procedures

### TGA (Therapeutic Goods Administration - Australia)

**Inclusion in ARTG (Australian Register of Therapeutic Goods):**
- Application for inclusion
- Conformity assessment certification (TGA assessment or overseas)
- Essential Principles checklist (equivalent to EU GSPR)
- Clinical evidence supporting safety and performance
- Quality Management System evidence (ISO 13485)
- Australian Sponsor responsibilities

**Documentation Requirements:**
- Technical file or design dossier
- Conformity assessment procedures
- Clinical evaluation report
- Post-market surveillance plan
- Labeling and IFU compliant with Australian standards

### Other International Markets

**PMDA (Japan):**
- Pre-market approval or notification (depending on class)
- Clinical data (may require Japan-specific data)
- QMS documentation (ISO 13485 or equivalent)
- Japanese language labeling
- Japanese marketing authorization holder (MAH)

**NMPA (China):**
- Registration certificate application
- Product technical requirements
- Clinical evaluation materials (may require China trials)
- Quality management system documentation
- Chinese language labeling and IFU
- China-based agent or subsidiary

**ANVISA (Brazil):**
- Registration application (ANVISA registration)
- Good Manufacturing Practice certificate
- Technical documentation (similar to EU MDR)
- Clinical evidence
- Portuguese language labeling

## Regulatory Submission Documentation

### Submission Package Assembly

**Cover Letter:**
- Device name and proposed classification
- Submission type and regulatory pathway
- Establishment registration number
- Contact information
- Request for meetings (if applicable)

**Table of Contents:**
- Organized per regulatory requirements
- Section and page number references
- Cross-reference to standards and guidance documents
- Index of all documents submitted

**Administrative Information:**
- Indications for use statement
- Device description (brief)
- Regulatory history
- Substantial equivalence statement (510(k))
- Truthful and accuracy statement

**Technical Sections:**
- Device description (detailed)
- Performance testing data
- Biocompatibility data
- Software documentation (if applicable)
- Sterilization validation (if applicable)
- Stability/shelf life data
- Clinical data (if required)
- Risk analysis summary
- Labeling (draft)

**Quality Documentation:**
- Manufacturing information
- Quality control procedures
- Sterilization and contamination control
- Facilities and equipment

**Supporting Documentation:**
- References to standards and guidance
- Literature references
- Certificates and declarations
- Financial disclosure (if clinical data)

### Submission Quality Assurance

**Pre-submission Checklist:**
- [ ] All required sections included
- [ ] Page numbering complete and accurate
- [ ] Cross-references correct
- [ ] Consistent terminology throughout
- [ ] All referenced documents included
- [ ] Signatures and dates present
- [ ] Electronic submission validated
- [ ] Cover letter addresses key points
- [ ] Requested meetings identified

**Technical Review:**
- Engineering review of technical content
- Quality review of test data and reports
- Regulatory review for compliance with guidance
- Management review and approval
- Final proofreading for accuracy

**Post-submission Management:**
- Submission tracking (date submitted, review timeline)
- Communication log with regulatory authority
- Additional information requests and responses
- Review status updates
- Approval or clearance documentation

## Post-Market Documentation

### Post-Market Surveillance

**PMS Plan Elements:**
- Objectives of surveillance activities
- Data collection methods (complaints, literature, registries, studies)
- Data analysis methods
- Frequency of PMS report generation
- Criteria for escalation and notification

**PMSR Content:**
- Sales and distribution data
- Summary of vigilance data (incidents, near misses)
- Summary of complaint data
- Summary of PMCF data
- Analysis of trends and patterns
- Conclusions on safety and performance
- Actions taken or planned

**PSUR (Periodic Safety Update Report) - EU MDR:**
- Consolidated analysis of PMS data
- Volume of sales and device population estimate
- Analysis of serious incidents and field safety corrective actions
- Summary of PMCF activities and results
- Overall conclusions on benefit-risk ratio
- Sales volume and device in circulation estimate

### Vigilance and Incident Reporting

**Medical Device Reporting (MDR) - FDA:**
- Reportable events (death, serious injury, malfunction)
- 5-day reports (death or serious injury with follow-up)
- 30-day reports (all reportable events)
- Baseline reports (establishing MDR reporting)
- Annual certification (no reportable events if applicable)

**Vigilance Reporting - EU MDR:**
- Serious incidents reported to competent authority within 15 days (varies by severity)
- Immediate notification for certain events (death, serious deterioration)
- Trend reporting (similar incidents indicating systematic problem)
- Final report after investigation completed

**Incident Investigation Documentation:**
- Initial incident report
- Investigation plan
- Evidence collection (device return, medical records, user interviews)
- Root cause analysis
- Corrective and preventive actions
- Effectiveness verification
- Final incident report and closure

### Field Safety Corrective Actions (FSCA)

**FSCA Documentation:**
- FSCA plan (actions to be taken)
- Field Safety Notice (FSN) to customers and users
- Customer acknowledgment and verification
- FSCA implementation records
- FSCA effectiveness verification
- Notification to regulatory authorities
- Completion report and closure

**Recall Documentation (if applicable):**
- Recall strategy and plan
- Customer notification
- Depth of recall (user level, distribution level, etc.)
- Recall status reports (weekly or as required)
- Recall effectiveness checks
- Recall termination request
- Recall completion report

## Inspection Documentation Requirements

### FDA Inspection Readiness

**Documents to Have Ready:**
- Quality manual and procedures
- Device Master Record (DMR)
- Representative Device History Records (DHR)
- Complaint files (organized and complete)
- CAPA records (investigation reports, action plans)
- Design History File (DHF) for recent products
- Supplier quality records
- Internal audit reports
- Management review records
- Training records
- Validation records (process, cleaning, software)
- Equipment calibration and maintenance records

**Inspection Room Setup:**
- Private room for inspector(s)
- Table and seating
- Power outlets and internet access (if permitted)
- Document retrieval system (request and provide process)
- Designated document control person
- Log of documents provided to inspector
- Photocopying or scanning capability

**Personnel Preparation:**
- Assign escort and subject matter experts
- Brief personnel on inspection protocol
- Review talking points (what to say and not say)
- Practice answering common questions
- Emphasize honesty and accuracy
- Instruct on escalation procedure for difficult questions

### Notified Body Audit (EU MDR)

**Audit Documentation Requirements:**
- QMS documentation (manual, procedures)
- Technical documentation for products in scope
- Risk management files
- Clinical evaluation reports and PMCF plans
- Design and development files
- Production and process validation records
- Supplier quality agreements and evaluations
- Complaint handling and vigilance procedures
- CAPA records and trends
- Management review records
- Training records and competency assessments
- Internal audit program and reports
- Certificates from suppliers or subcontractors

**Audit Types:**
- Initial certification audit (Stage 1 and Stage 2)
- Surveillance audits (annual or biennial)
- Recertification audits (every 3 years typically)
- Unannounced audits (for certain classifications)
- Extension audits (adding products or sites)

**Audit Response:**
- Nonconformity reports (NCR) and corrective action plans
- Objective evidence of implementation
- Effectiveness verification
- Timeline for closure
- Follow-up audit if major nonconformities

### ISO 13485 Certification Audit

**Audit Scope:**
- All ISO 13485 clauses applicable to organization
- All processes and functional areas
- Multiple sites if applicable
- Outsourced processes

**Stage 1 Audit (Documentation Review):**
- QMS documentation review
- Readiness assessment
- Identification of gaps
- Planning for Stage 2 audit

**Stage 2 Audit (Implementation Verification):**
- On-site verification of QMS implementation
- Interviews with personnel
- Document and record review
- Observation of processes
- Verification of effectiveness

**Audit Findings:**
- Major nonconformities (prevent certification or cause suspension)
- Minor nonconformities (require corrective action)
- Observations or opportunities for improvement
- Corrective action plans with timelines
- Verification of corrective action closure

**Surveillance Audits:**
- Annual or biennial (per certification body)
- Focus on high-risk areas and changes
- Verification of previous corrective actions
- Re-verification of QMS effectiveness

## Best Practices for Regulatory Documentation

**Documentation Quality:**
1. Clear, concise, and technically accurate
2. Consistent terminology throughout
3. Complete traceability and cross-referencing
4. Version control and change management
5. Review and approval by competent personnel

**Regulatory Submission Excellence:**
1. Follow regulatory guidance documents
2. Pre-submission meetings when appropriate
3. Anticipate regulatory questions proactively
4. High-quality test data and reports
5. Clear and compliant labeling

**Inspection and Audit Readiness:**
1. Maintain documentation current and accessible
2. Regular internal audits and mock inspections
3. CAPA system robust and effective
4. Training and competency well documented
5. Transparency and cooperation with authorities

**Common Documentation Pitfalls:**
1. Incomplete or missing documentation
2. Inconsistent or outdated procedures
3. Poor traceability and cross-referencing
4. Inadequate change control
5. Insufficient review and approval
6. Records not retained per requirements
7. Electronic system not validated (21 CFR Part 11)
